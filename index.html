Welcome to CU Apex 👋

A quick orientation before you run your first quarter

1 What is CU Apex?

CU Apex puts you in the executive chair of a real credit union — Lakeside Community Credit Union, seeded from actual NCUA call report data with $750M in assets.

Your team of four will make real management decisions each quarter and compete against other teams on a regulator-calibrated scorecard.

🏦 Your Institution This Session Lakeside Community CU · $750M Assets · 48,240 Members · Net Worth: 16.2% (well-capitalized)
Scenario: "The Great Unwind" — Rising rate environment, Q2 2023

2 Your Team's Four Roles

Each team member has a specific executive role with its own decisions and private information. No single role sees everything.

🟢 CEO — Strategy

Sets capital targets, growth posture, and staffing. Only role that sees all four executive summaries.

🔵 CFO — Investments & Funding

Controls the investment portfolio and deposit pricing. Sees full ALM risk data others don't receive.

🟡 CLO — Lending

Sets loan volumes, rates, and credit standards. Only role with full delinquency detail by vintage.

🟣 CMO — Members

Grows membership and protects deposits. Receives the private Member Satisfaction Index each quarter.

3 How a Round Works

Each round represents one simulated quarter (3 months). Here's what happens:

  • Economic Briefing — New conditions published in the CU News Wire. Read it — it affects your decisions.
  • Each role submits decisions — Use the sliders and toggles in each role's tab.
  • CEO reviews and processes — Hit ⚡ Process Quarter to run the simulation.
  • Results published — New financial statements, CAMEL scores, and leaderboard revealed.
📌 In This Tutorial All four role tabs are unlocked — explore them all. Adjust sliders, then click ⚡ Process Quarter on the CEO tab to run the simulation engine and see instant results.

4 How You're Scored

Your team earns an Apex Score (0–100) each quarter using the CAMEL framework your real examiner uses.

  • C — Capital (25%): Is your net worth ratio strong and growing?
  • A — Asset Quality (20%): Are your loans performing?
  • M — Management (20%): Are you operating efficiently?
  • E — Earnings (20%): Is the credit union profitable vs. peers?
  • L — Liquidity (15%): Can you meet obligations?
⚠ The Most Important Rule If your Net Worth Ratio falls below 7.0%, your team is placed in Prompt Corrective Action and loses growth authority. Survival before growth.
